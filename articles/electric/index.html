Electricity delivery

Large power plants generate the electricity that homes use. Electricity is produced by power plants across the nation using generators that are powered by steam, water, or wind. This power goes into massive “step-up” transformers that boost the voltage to half a million volts or higher.

The power is subsequently reduced for distribution down the street lines by “step-down” transformers installed at distribution substations. A series of smaller transformers mounted on utility power poles further lower the voltage to the standard 120 volts needed by most homes.
